The Ruins: A Love Story in Stone and Fire

Nestled in the heart of Talisay City, Negros Occidental, Philippines, stands a magnificent structure that has become one of the most iconic tourist destinations in the region—The Ruins. This enchanting place, captured beautifully in the image, offers not just a glimpse of architectural brilliance but also a window into the past, with a romantic story woven into its history. A Mansion of Love and Loss The Ruins, often referred to as the "Taj Mahal of Negros," was once a grand mansion built by Don Mariano Ledesma Lacson in the early 1900s. The mansion was a tribute to his beloved wife, Maria Braga, a Portuguese woman from Macau. After Maria's untimely death during the birth of their eleventh child, Don Mariano built the mansion as a symbol of his enduring love for her. The mansion became a testament to their love and the grandeur of a bygone era.

A Love Etched in Time

The story of The Ruins begins with Don Mariano Ledesma Lacson, a wealthy sugar baron in the early 1900s. When his wife Maria Braga, a beautiful Portuguese woman, passed away, Don Mariano decided to build a mansion in her memory. The grand structure stood as a monument to their love.

A Mansion Set Ablaze

However, during World War II, the mansion's fate took a tragic turn. To prevent its use by Japanese forces, Filipino guerillas burned it to the ground. The fire consumed the house for days, leaving only the sturdy concrete structure behind—a haunting reminder of the past.

A Tourist's Dream

A beautiful garden surrounds The Ruins, offering visitors a peaceful escape. The Ruins is more than just a historical site. It’s a place where visitors can stroll through the lush gardens, bask in the serene atmosphere, and even host special events like weddings. The Romanesque architecture of the mansion’s facade, illuminated at night, gives it a timeless charm
